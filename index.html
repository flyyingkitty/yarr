1. INCIDENT SUMMARY

This report details an uncontrolled temporal displacement event (TDE) originating at Site Gamma-7 (Nevada Test and Training Range, Restricted Sector) at 09:17 PST, 19 JUL 2024. The event occurred during calibration tests of the Chronometric Resonance Field Generator (CRFG) Unit 03. An unexpected energy cascade resulted in the formation of a localized temporal aperture.

  • **Equipment:** Mobile Sensor Array (MSA-04), portable power unit, emergency survival kits (72-hour).

Last telemetry from personnel bio-monitors ceased at 09:17:32 PST, concurrent with peak energy readings from the CRFG.

3. DESTINATION ANALYSIS (PRELIMINARY)

A micro-second data burst was received from MSA-04 immediately prior to signal loss. Analysis of atmospheric composition, radiation levels, and preliminary paleomagnetic readings strongly suggest a temporal displacement to the Mesozoic Era, specifically the late Cretaceous period (approx. 68-66 Million Years BP).

Observed environmental markers from the data burst include: significantly elevated CO2 levels, high humidity, dominant flora (ferns, cycads, early angiosperms), and detection of large, unidentified biological masses consistent with saurian life forms.

4. PRIMARY CONCERNS & IMPLICATIONS

  • Personnel Safety & Recovery: The immediate welfare of Expeditionary Team Delta is paramount. The destination environment is presumed extremely hostile.

  • Technological Security: Loss of advanced equipment (MSA-04) in a non-secure temporal zone is a critical concern.
  • Public Disclosure Prevention: The nature of this incident necessitates absolute information containment.

  2. Initiate continuous attempts to re-establish communication with Expeditionary Team Delta via experimental tachyon pulse (Project ECHO).

Mesozoic Target Zone (MTZ) Assessment - Preliminary (Based on MSA-04 Burst)

Data received from Mobile Sensor Array (MSA-04) prior to signal loss indicates displacement to a terrestrial environment consistent with Earth's Late Cretaceous Period.

  • Atmospheric Composition:
    • Oxygen: ~26-30% (Higher than present)
    • Carbon Dioxide: ~1000-1500 ppm (Significantly elevated)
    • Nitrogen: Dominant, similar to present levels.
  • Ambient Temperature (Est.): 25-30°C (Local reading, likely tropical/subtropical)
  • Radiation Levels: Background solar/cosmic radiation within expected parameters for the period. No anomalous localized radiation sources detected in the brief scan.
  • Dominant Flora (Visual/Spectral): Dense vegetation. Strong signatures of ferns, cycads, conifers, and early flowering plants (angiosperms). No grasses detected.
  • Fauna (Acoustic/Seismic/Thermal):
    • Multiple large (est. >500kg) thermal signatures detected within 1km radius.
    • Low-frequency vocalizations and heavy ground tremors recorded, consistent with large terrestrial vertebrates.
    • Caution: MSA-04 passive sensors registered rapid movement of a large predatory form (est. length 9-12m) approaching the MSA's last known position.

Conclusion: Environment is viable for human short-term survival with appropriate protection but presents extreme biological hazards.
